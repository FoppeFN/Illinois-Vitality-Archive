Glossary

Definitions for common terms used throughout the Illinois Vitality Archive.

Birth Record
An official document registering the birth of a person. Typically includes the individual's full name, date of birth, place of birth, and parents' names.
Death Record
An official document registering the death of a person. Typically includes the individual's full name, date of death, place of death, and cause of death.
Marriage Record
An official document registering the marriage of two individuals. Typically includes the names of both parties, the date of marriage, and the county where the marriage took place.
Vital Records
Records of life events kept under governmental authority, including births, deaths, marriages, and divorces. Vital records are a primary source for genealogical research.
County
A political and geographic subdivision of a state. Illinois is divided into 102 counties, each maintaining its own local vital records.
+/- Year Search
A year range search that returns records within a specified number of years of the entered date. For example, searching for birth year 1924 with a variance of ±5 will return all records with a birth year between 1919 and 1929. This is useful when the exact year of an event is uncertain or when historical records may contain minor dating errors. Records in this archive span birth years from 1821 to 1960 and death years from 1845 to 2062.
Fuzzy Search
A name matching method that finds records even when the spelling is approximate or imprecise. Rather than requiring an exact match, fuzzy search uses trigram similarity to identify names that look or sound similar to what was entered. This is especially helpful when searching historical records where names may have been recorded inconsistently or misspelled differently over time.
