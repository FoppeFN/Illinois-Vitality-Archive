Glossary

Definitions for common terms used throughout the Illinois Vitality Archive.

Birth Record
An official document registering the birth of a person. Typically includes the individual's full name, date of birth, place of birth, and parents' names.
Death Record
An official document registering the death of a person. Typically includes the individual's full name, date of death, place of death, and cause of death.
Marriage Record
An official document registering the marriage of two individuals. Typically includes the names of both parties, the date of marriage, and the county where the marriage took place.
Vital Records
Records of life events kept under governmental authority, including births, deaths, marriages, and divorces. Vital records are a primary source for genealogical research.
County
A political and geographic subdivision of a state. Illinois is divided into 102 counties, each maintaining its own local vital records.
Genealogy
The study of families, family history, and the tracing of lineages. Vital records are among the most important primary sources used in genealogical research.
Index
A searchable list of records that allows users to locate specific entries within a larger database. IVA provides an index of vital records searchable by name, date, and county.
